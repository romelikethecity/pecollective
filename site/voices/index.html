Top 25 Prompt Engineering Voices of 2026

The most influential prompt engineers, AI engineers, and educators shaping how professionals work with AI

Last updated: 2026-04-14 · 25 voices ranked

How We Ranked These Voices

Ranked by cross-list appearances, community impact, published work, and relevance to prompt engineering and AI engineering. Sources include DAIR.AI, AI education platforms, LLM tooling communities, and independent analysis. We evaluated 50+ candidates and filtered for people who actively advance how professionals interact with AI systems.

  • Topic relevance (required): Must actively contribute to prompt engineering or AI engineering.
  • Published work (30%): Open-source tools, guides, courses, books that advance the discipline.
  • Community impact (25%): Building communities, teaching, open-source contributions.
  • Content reach (25%): Newsletter subscribers, GitHub stars, LinkedIn following.
  • Originality (20%): Original techniques, frameworks, and tools.

Top 10 Leaders

The most recognized voices shaping prompt engineering and AI engineering.

#1

Riley Goodside

Staff Prompt Engineer at Scale AI

Prompt PioneerScale AIPrompt Injection

The original prompt engineer. First person to hold the 'Staff Prompt Engineer' title at Scale AI. His early explorations of GPT-3 prompt injection, jailbreaking, and creative prompting techniques on Twitter defined the field before it had a name. Every prompt engineering technique traces some lineage back to his experiments.

#2

Simon Willison

Creator & Developer at Datasette / LLM CLI

LLM ToolingDatasetteDjango Co-Creator

Creator of Datasette and the LLM command-line tool. Co-creator of Django. His blog is the most comprehensive technical resource on working with LLMs in practice. Covers prompt engineering, tool use, RAG, and AI safety with a depth and rigor that no one else matches. The technical conscience of the prompt engineering community.

#3

Elvis Saravia

Founder at DAIR.AI / Prompt Engineering Guide

PE GuideDAIR.AI50K GitHub Stars

Created the Prompt Engineering Guide (promptingguide.ai), the most-starred and most-referenced prompt engineering resource on GitHub (50K+ stars). Founded DAIR.AI to democratize AI research and education. His guide is the curriculum that thousands of practitioners learned prompt engineering from.

#4

Lilian Weng

Head of Safety Systems at OpenAI

OpenAILLM AgentsTechnical Blog

Head of Safety Systems at OpenAI. Her blog (lilianweng.github.io) is the most technically rigorous resource on LLM agents, prompt engineering patterns, and AI systems design. Posts like 'Prompt Engineering' and 'LLM Powered Autonomous Agents' are canonical references cited in research papers and production systems alike.

#5

Andrej Karpathy

Founder & Educator at Eureka Labs (Ex-Tesla AI, Ex-OpenAI)

EducatorEx-Tesla AIEx-OpenAI

Former Director of AI at Tesla and founding member of OpenAI. Now building Eureka Labs for AI education. His YouTube lectures on neural networks and LLMs have millions of views. His 'Let's build GPT from scratch' video is the single best resource for understanding how the models behind prompt engineering actually work.

#6

Ethan Mollick

Professor & Author at Wharton School

AuthorWhartonAI + Work Research

Wharton professor and author of 'Co-Intelligence.' His Substack 'One Useful Thing' is where non-technical professionals learn how to prompt effectively. His research on AI's impact on knowledge work showed that prompting skill is the key differentiator in AI-augmented performance.

#7

Swyx (Shawn Wang)

Founder at Latent Space / smol.ai

AI EngineeringLatent SpacePodcasting

Runs the Latent Space podcast and newsletter covering AI engineering. Coined 'AI Engineer' as a role category. His content on building AI applications, prompt chains, and agent architectures bridges the gap between prompt engineering and software engineering.

#8

Harrison Chase

CEO & Co-Founder at LangChain

LangChainLLM FrameworkFounder

Built LangChain, the most popular framework for building LLM applications. While not a 'prompt engineer' by title, LangChain's abstractions for prompt templates, chains, and agents defined how the industry structures prompt-driven applications. The tooling that made prompt engineering scalable.

#9

Sander Schulhoff

Researcher & Author at Learn Prompting

Learn PromptingResearchEducation

Created Learn Prompting (learnprompting.org), one of the most comprehensive free prompt engineering courses. Lead author of 'The Prompt Report,' a systematic review of prompting techniques across 1,500+ papers. His academic approach to cataloging prompt techniques brought rigor to a field that was mostly vibes.

#10

Jeremy Howard

Co-Founder at fast.ai / Answer.AI

fast.aiEducationAnswer.AI

Co-founded fast.ai (making deep learning accessible) and Answer.AI. Former President and Chief Scientist at Kaggle. His practical AI education philosophy shaped how an entire generation learned ML. Now focused on making AI tools work for everyday users through Answer.AI.

Jason Liu

Creator at Instructor (Python Library)

InstructorStructured OutputPython

Created Instructor, the most popular library for structured output from LLMs. His work on making LLMs return reliable, typed data instead of free text solved one of the biggest practical problems in prompt engineering. Active teacher on prompt design patterns.

Newsletter2M SubscribersAI Tools

Philipp Schmid
