Top 25 Prompt Engineering Voices of 2026

The most influential prompt engineers, AI engineers, and educators shaping how professionals work with AI

Last updated: 2026-04-14 · 25 voices ranked

How We Ranked These Voices

Ranked by cross-list appearances, community impact, published work, and relevance to prompt engineering and AI engineering. Sources include DAIR.AI, Anthropic team tracking, OpenAI ecosystem, DeepLearning.AI, Learn Prompting, LangChain community, and independent analysis. We evaluated 50+ candidates and filtered for people who actively advance how professionals interact with AI systems.

  • Topic relevance (required): Must actively contribute to prompt engineering or AI engineering.
  • Published work (30%): Open-source tools, guides, courses, books that advance the discipline.
  • Community impact (25%): Building communities, teaching, open-source contributions.
  • Content reach (25%): Newsletter subscribers, GitHub stars, LinkedIn following.
  • Originality (20%): Original techniques, frameworks, and tools.

Top 10 Leaders

The most recognized voices shaping prompt engineering and AI engineering.

#1

Riley Goodside

Staff Prompt Engineer at Google DeepMind (Ex-Scale AI)

Prompt PioneerDeepMindRed-Teaming

The original prompt engineer and first person to hold the 'Staff Prompt Engineer' title at Scale AI. His early explorations of GPT-3 prompt injection, jailbreaking, and creative prompting on Twitter defined the field before it had a name. Now at Google DeepMind. Every prompt engineering technique traces some lineage back to his experiments.

#2

Simon Willison

Creator & Developer at Datasette / LLM CLI

LLM ToolingDjango Co-Creator150+ Tools

Creator of Datasette and the LLM command-line tool. Django co-creator. Built 150+ HTML tools for working with LLMs. His blog at simonwillison.net is the most comprehensive technical resource on working with LLMs in practice. Covers prompt engineering, tool use, RAG, and AI safety with a depth and rigor no one else matches.

Elvis Saravia

Co-Founder at DAIR.AI / Prompt Engineering Guide

PE Guide50K GitHub Stars3M Learners

Created the Prompt Engineering Guide (promptingguide.ai), the most-starred prompt engineering resource on GitHub (50K+ stars) that has reached 3M+ learners. Founded DAIR.AI. Former Meta AI / Papers with Code. Teaches advanced PE courses on Maven. The curriculum thousands of practitioners learned prompt engineering from.

#4

Amanda Askell

Head of Alignment Finetuning at Anthropic

AnthropicAlignmentConstitutional AI

Philosopher-turned-AI-researcher who leads Claude's personality alignment at Anthropic. Key contributor to Constitutional AI. Known as 'Anthropic's resident philosopher' for prompt engineering. PhD from NYU, former OpenAI. Her work literally shapes how Claude responds to prompts at the model level.

#5

Lilian Weng

Head of Safety Systems at OpenAI

OpenAILLM AgentsCanonical Blog

Head of Safety Systems at OpenAI. Her blog (lilianweng.github.io) is the most technically rigorous resource on LLM agents, prompt engineering patterns, and AI systems design. Posts like 'Prompt Engineering' and 'LLM Powered Autonomous Agents' are canonical references cited in research papers and production systems alike.

#6

Andrej Karpathy

Founder & Educator at Eureka Labs (Ex-Tesla AI, Ex-OpenAI)

EducatorEx-Tesla AIMillions of Views

Former Director of AI at Tesla and founding member of OpenAI. Now building Eureka Labs for AI education. His YouTube lectures on neural networks and LLMs have millions of views. 'Let's build GPT from scratch' is the single best resource for understanding how the models behind prompt engineering actually work.

#7

Sander Schulhoff

CEO at Learn Prompting / HackAPrompt

Learn PromptingHackAPromptPrompt Report

Created Learn Prompting (learnprompting.org), the first prompt engineering guide online, before ChatGPT launched. Runs HackAPrompt, the world's largest AI red-teaming competition (600K+ prompts). Lead author of 'The Prompt Report,' a 76-page systematic review with OpenAI, Microsoft, Google, Princeton, and Stanford researchers.

#8

Harrison Chase

CEO & Co-Founder at LangChain

LangChainLLM FrameworkAgent Orchestration

Built LangChain, the most popular framework for building LLM applications, plus LangGraph and LangSmith. Harvard stats/CS graduate. Prompt chaining and agent orchestration pioneer. Professor & Author at Wharton School

Senior Prompt Engineer at Anthropic

AnthropicPrompt DoctorWorkshops

Known as the 'Prompt Doctor' at Anthropic. Leads hands-on Claude prompt workshops at conferences. MS in NLP from Northwestern. His practical demonstrations of advanced prompting techniques for Claude give practitioners the closest thing to an official masterclass from the model maker.
