Frequently Asked Questions

What is prompt engineering?

Prompt engineering is the practice of designing inputs for AI models like GPT-4 and Claude to get reliable, useful outputs. It's part writing, part logic, part experimentation. Our prompt engineering guide covers the core techniques, from zero-shot prompting to chain-of-thought reasoning.

How do I get started as a prompt engineer?

Start by learning the fundamentals. Our complete guide walks you through core techniques, and our best practices article covers what actually works in production. Then practice. A lot. Build a portfolio of prompts that solve real problems.

What do prompt engineers earn?

Based on data from our community and job board, prompt engineers in the US earn between $120k and $250k depending on experience and company. Check our salary data for current numbers broken down by role, location, and experience level.

Is prompt engineering a real career?

Yes. Companies like Anthropic, OpenAI, Google, and hundreds of startups actively hire prompt engineers. Our job board tracks 200+ active AI roles with real salary data. The field is still young, but demand is growing fast as more companies deploy AI products.
