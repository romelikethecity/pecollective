Best Embedding Models 2026 - Benchmarks, Pricing & When to Use Each

By Rome Thorndike · April 7, 2026 · 10 min read

Embedding models convert text into numerical vectors that capture semantic meaning. They power RAG systems, semantic search, and recommendation engines. Picking the right embedding model affects retrieval quality, cost, and latency. This guide compares every major option as of April 2026.

How to Choose an Embedding Model

For production RAG systems

Use Voyage voyage-3-large or Cohere embed-v4. Both score highest on MTEB retrieval benchmarks, which directly measure how well embeddings find relevant documents. Voyage has a longer context window (32K tokens) which helps with longer documents. Cohere is cheaper per token.

For cost-sensitive applications

Use OpenAI text-embedding-3-small at $0.02/1M tokens or Voyage voyage-3-lite at $0.02/1M tokens. Both deliver solid performance at 100x less cost than the best models. For many use cases, the quality difference is negligible.

For self-hosted / privacy-sensitive applications

Use BGE-M3 or GTE-Qwen2. Both are open source with strong MTEB scores. BGE-M3 supports multi-language and multi-granularity retrieval. GTE-Qwen2 offers a 32K context window. Running these requires GPU infrastructure but eliminates API costs and keeps data on-premise.

For prototyping

Use Google Gemini Embedding via AI Studio (free) or OpenAI text-embedding-3-small ($0.02/1M). Both are easy to set up and cheap enough for experimentation.

MTEB Benchmarks Explained

MTEB (Massive Text Embedding Benchmark) evaluates embedding models across multiple tasks: retrieval, classification, clustering, pair classification, reranking, and STS (semantic textual similarity). The "Average" score shown above is the mean across all tasks.

For RAG applications, the retrieval subscore matters most. Voyage voyage-3-large and Cohere embed-v4 lead on retrieval specifically, even though their overall MTEB averages are close to other models.

Dimension Size Trade-offs

Higher dimensions capture more nuance but cost more to store and search. Here is the practical impact:

  • 512 dimensions: ~2KB per vector. Good for most use cases. 1M vectors = ~2GB in Pinecone or pgvector.
  • 1,024 dimensions: ~4KB per vector. Better for nuanced retrieval. 1M vectors = ~4GB.
  • 3,072 dimensions: ~12KB per vector. Marginal improvement over 1,024 for most tasks. 1M vectors = ~12GB. Storage and search costs increase significantly.

OpenAI's text-embedding-3 models support dimension reduction via the dimensions parameter. You can generate 3,072-dimension embeddings but truncate to 1,024 or 512, getting most of the quality at lower storage cost. This is the best of both worlds if you use OpenAI.
