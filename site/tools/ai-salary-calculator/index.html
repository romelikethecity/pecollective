How We Calculated This

Methodology

This calculator uses salary data from PE Collective's database of 1,300+ AI and ML job postings collected between December 2025 and February 2026. We analyze base salary ranges by role, experience level, and location, then apply adjustments for company type and in-demand skills.

Salary figures represent base compensation only. Total compensation at Big Tech and well-funded startups can be 30-60% higher when including equity, bonuses, and benefits. International salaries are converted to USD equivalent.

These estimates are directional. Your actual offer depends on negotiation, company stage, funding, team budget, and dozens of other factors we can't measure from a job posting.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the average prompt engineer salary in 2026?

Based on PE Collective's analysis of 1,300+ job postings, the median prompt engineer salary in 2026 is $145,000 per year. Entry-level positions start around $90,000-$110,000, mid-level roles pay $120,000-$160,000, and senior prompt engineers earn $160,000-$250,000+. San Francisco and New York pay the highest, with remote roles typically coming in 5-15% lower than top-market rates.

How much do AI engineers make compared to software engineers?

AI engineers earn roughly 10-20% more than traditional software engineers at the same experience level. A mid-level software engineer in SF might earn $160,000-$200,000, while a mid-level AI engineer at the same company earns $180,000-$230,000. This premium reflects specialized skills in ML, LLMs, and production AI systems that are in short supply relative to demand.

What skills increase my AI salary the most?

The highest-premium skills in 2026 AI roles are: multi-agent system design (+15-25% premium), RAG architecture experience (+15-20%), AI safety and red teaming (+10-20%), and production ML deployment experience (+10-15%). Python is table stakes and doesn't command a premium on its own. Research publications and fine-tuning experience also carry meaningful premiums, especially at AI research labs.

Does this calculator include equity and bonuses?

No, this calculator estimates base salary only. Total compensation varies dramatically by company type. At Big Tech companies, equity and bonuses can add 30-60% on top of base. Well-funded AI startups may offer significant equity (though its value is uncertain). Enterprise companies lean heavier on base salary with smaller bonus percentages. When comparing offers, always look at total comp, not just base.
