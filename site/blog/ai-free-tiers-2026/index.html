AI Free Tiers 2026 - What You Get for $0 on Every Major Platform

By Rome Thorndike · April 7, 2026 · 10 min read

Every major AI platform offers a free tier. But what you actually get varies enormously. Some free tiers are genuinely useful for daily work. Others are glorified demos that push you to upgrade within hours. This page compares every major AI free tier as of April 2026.

Which Free Tier Is Actually Useful?

Best overall free tier: Google Gemini

Google offers the most generous free tier across both consumer (Gemini app) and developer (AI Studio) products. You get access to Gemini 2.5 Pro and Flash through AI Studio with reasonable rate limits, and the Gemini app includes most features for free. If you are exploring AI and don't want to pay anything, start here.

Best free coding tool: GitHub Copilot

With 2,000 completions and 50 chat messages per month, Copilot's free tier is the most usable for actual coding work. The other editors (Cursor, Windsurf) have more restrictive free tiers that run out quickly during a normal coding session.

Best free API: Google AI Studio

AI Studio's free tier is the most practical for developers building prototypes. The 15 RPM on Gemini 2.5 Flash is enough to build and test applications without paying. OpenAI and Anthropic's $5 credits expire and don't renew.

When to Upgrade from Free

Free tiers work for exploration and occasional use. Upgrade when:

  • You hit rate limits daily and your work depends on AI
  • You need features like extended thinking (Claude Pro), Composer/Agent (Cursor Pro), or Copilot Workspace
  • You are building a product and need reliable API access with higher throughput
  • You are a professional developer coding 20+ hours/week with AI assistance

The sweet spot for most people: use free tiers for chat (Gemini or Claude) and pay for one coding tool (Copilot at $10/mo is the cheapest entry point).
